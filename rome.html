Rome


The Eternal City:


Rome, the capital of Italy, is one of the world's most historically and culturally rich cities.
Known as "The Eternal City," it has been a center of power, culture, and religion for over two millennia.
Its blend of ancient ruins, Renaissance art, and vibrant modern life attracts millions of visitors every year.
Here’s a description of Rome and its most iconic landmarks:




Colosseum:

The Colosseum is an ancient amphitheater that once hosted gladiator games and public spectacles.
Built in AD 70-80, it could hold up to 50,000 spectators.
As one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, it remains a powerful symbol of Rome's grandeur.



St. Peter’s Basilica:

Located in Vatican City, St. Peter’s Basilica is one of the largest and most important churches in Christianity.
Its stunning dome, designed by Michelangelo, offers breathtaking views of Rome.
The interior features masterpieces like Michelangelo's Pietà.



Vatican Museums and Sistine Chapel:

The Vatican Museums house one of the world's most significant art collections, including ancient sculptures and Renaissance masterpieces.
The Sistine Chapel, famous for Michelangelo's ceiling frescoes, is a highlight of the museum tour.
