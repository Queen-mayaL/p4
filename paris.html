The City of Light:


Paris, the capital city of France, is known as the "City of Light" for its pivotal role in art, culture, and philosophy during the Age of Enlightenment. It's a global hub for fashion, food, and history, attracting millions of visitors every year. The city's charming streets, artistic heritage, and romantic ambiance make it one of the most beloved destinations in the world. Here are some of its most famous landmarks:




Eiffel Tower:

The Eiffel Tower is Paris's most iconic landmark. Built for the 1889 World’s Fair, it stands 324 meters tall and offers breathtaking views of the city.
Visitors can ascend to its observation decks via stairs or elevators to marvel at the skyline.



Louvre Museum:

The Louvre is the largest art museum in the world and a historic monument.
Once a royal palace, it now houses thousands of artworks, including the famous Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, and Winged Victory of Samothrace.
Its glass pyramid entrance is a modern architectural marvel.



Notre-Dame Cathedral:

This masterpiece of French Gothic architecture, located on the Île de la Cité, is famous for its stunning rose windows, towering spires, and intricate sculptures.
Though damaged by a fire in 2019, its restoration continues to honor its legacy as a cultural and religious icon.
